## PVC COMPONENTS (~$25)

### Main Tube - MOST IMPORTANT!

**Option 1: Single 10-foot piece (BEST)**
- **Product:** 4" PVC Pipe, Schedule 40, 10 ft length
- **Where:** Home Depot, Lowe's, or local plumbing supply
- **Price:** $15-25
- **Part #:** Varies by store
- **Notes:** 
  - 10 ft = 3048mm (close enough to 3000mm!)
  - Ask them to cut to 3000mm if possible
  - Schedule 40 is standard wall thickness
  - White or gray both work

**Option 2: Two 6-foot pieces**
- **Product:** 4" PVC Pipe, Schedule 40, 6 ft lengths (Ã—2)
- **Price:** $10-15 each
- **Notes:**
  - Will need to join - buy coupling!
  - Cut one to make total 3000mm
  - Seal joint VERY well

**Alternatives:**
- 3" diameter: Works fine, cheaper (~$10)
- 6" diameter: Works fine, heavier, more expensive (~$30)
- **Recommendation: Use whatever you have!**

---

### End Cap

**Product:** 4" PVC End Cap, slip-fit
- **Where:** Home Depot, Lowe's
- **Price:** $3-5
- **Notes:**
  - Match your pipe diameter
  - Slip-fit (slides on, then glue)
  - Get 2 if you want backup

---

### PVC Cement

**Product:** Oatey PVC Cement + Primer (purple)
- **Where:** Any hardware store
- **Price:** $8-12 for combo pack
- **Notes:**
  - Small can is enough
  - Purple primer (optional but recommended)
  - Clear cement (main adhesive)
  - Strong fumes - use outdoors!

---

### Optional: Coupling (if using multiple pieces)

**Product:** 4" PVC Coupling, slip-fit
- **Price:** $3-5
- **Notes:**
  - Only if tube comes in sections
  - Seal with cement as above

## AUDIO COMPONENTS (~$30-50)

### Speaker

**Budget Option (~$15):**
- **Product:** AmazonBasics USB-Powered Computer Speakers
- **Link:** Search "cheap computer speakers" on Amazon
- **Specs:** 3-4", USB powered, 2-5W
- **Notes:**
  - Use just one speaker
  - 3.5mm aux input
  - Frequency response usually 100-20000 Hz (plenty)

**Better Option (~$25-30):**
- **Product:** Pyle 4" Car Audio Speaker
- **Where:** Amazon, car audio stores
- **Model:** Any 4" aftermarket speaker
- **Specs:** 
  - 4" diameter (fits perfect in tube!)
  - 50-60W max (don't need full power)
  - Frequency response 80-20000 Hz
- **Notes:**
  - Need separate amplifier or
  - Connect to car stereo / old home stereo

**Professional Option (~$50-100):**
- **Product:** Dayton Audio full-range driver
- **Where:** Parts Express, Amazon
- **Model:** Dayton Audio DC28F-8, 1" dome tweeter
- **Specs:** Wide bandwidth, very flat response
- **Notes:** Overkill for first test but excellent quality

**RECOMMENDATION: Start with cheap computer speaker!**

---

### Microphone

**Budget Option (~$10-15):**
- **Product:** Any cheap USB microphone
- **Where:** Amazon, Best Buy, Walmart
- **Examples:**
  - "USB Microphone for PC"
  - Generic clip-on desktop mics
- **Notes:**
  - Just needs to record and fit in tube
  - Quality doesn't matter much for this test
  - Check that it's small enough (< 2" diameter)

**Better Option (~$30-50):**
- **Product:** Blue Snowball iCE USB Microphone
- **Where:** Amazon, Best Buy
- **Price:** ~$40-50
- **Notes:**
  - Good quality
  - Might be too large for tube interior
  - Could mount outside and use probe

**Lavalier Option (~$15-20):**
- **Product:** Clip-on lavalier mic with 3.5mm jack
- **Where:** Amazon
- **Examples:** "Lavalier lapel microphone"
- **Notes:**
  - PERFECT - tiny, lightweight
  - Easy to attach to probe
  - Use with USB adapter or laptop mic jack

**RECOMMENDATION: Lavalier mic or cheap USB mic**

---

### Cables

**Audio Cable for Speaker:**
- **Product:** 3.5mm aux cable, 6-10 ft
- **Where:** Anywhere
- **Price:** $5-10
- **Notes:**
  - Connects phone/laptop to speaker
  - Longer is better for positioning

**USB Cable for Microphone:**
- **Product:** Usually included with mic
- **Price:** $5 if need to buy
- **Notes:**
  - USB-A to micro-USB or USB-C
  - Need long enough to reach computer

**Speaker Wire (if using car speaker):**
- **Product:** 16-gauge speaker wire, 10 ft
- **Price:** $5
- **Notes:**
  - Red/black pair
  - Connect speaker to amp

**Optional: USB Extension:**
- **Product:** USB extension cable, 10 ft
- **Price:** $8-10
- **Notes:**
  - If mic cable too short
  - USB-A male to female

## MOUNTING MATERIALS (~$15)

### Foam for Speaker Gasket

**Product:** Weather stripping foam, self-adhesive
- **Where:** Hardware store
- **Price:** $5-8
- **Thickness:** 1/4" to 1/2"
- **Notes:**
  - Cut to shape
  - Seals speaker to tube
  - Also works for damping

**Alternative:** Craft foam sheet
- **Where:** Craft store, Amazon
- **Price:** $3-5
- **Notes:** Works fine, not adhesive

---

### Microphone Probe

**Option 1: Wooden Dowel**
- **Product:** Wooden dowel, 1/4" diameter, 10 ft length
- **Where:** Hardware store, craft store
- **Price:** $3-5
- **Notes:**
  - Thin enough to slide in tube
  - Long enough for full length
  - Cut to size if longer

**Option 2: Thin PVC Pipe**
- **Product:** 1/2" PVC pipe, Schedule 40
- **Where:** Hardware store
- **Price:** $5-8 for 10 ft
- **Notes:**
  - Stiffer than wood
  - Can thread cable through interior
  - Hollow allows wire routing

**Option 3: Fishing Rod Blank**
- **Product:** Telescoping fishing rod blank
- **Where:** Fishing supply, Amazon
- **Price:** $10-20
- **Notes:**
  - Collapsible for storage
  - Very stiff
  - Professional look

**RECOMMENDATION: Wooden dowel (cheapest, works great)**

## MONEY-SAVING TIPS

**1. Check what you have:**
- Old computer speakers? Use them!
- USB mic lying around? Perfect!
- Scrap PVC in garage? Measure it!

**2. Borrow tools:**
- Ask neighbor for tape measure
- Use phone for level/ruler
- Library might loan tools

**3. Buy generic:**
- Store-brand PVC cement works
- Cheapest foam is fine
- Generic cables are identical

**4. Start minimal:**
- One $10 mic is enough
- $15 speaker works great
- Can upgrade later

**5. Skip non-essentials:**
- Don't need acoustic foam
- Don't need multiple mics
- Don't need fancy mounts

**Absolute minimum: ~$60**
- $20 PVC pipe
- $4 end cap
- $10 cement
- $15 speaker + mic combo
- $6 cable
- $5 tape
(Use phone for white noise, Audacity for recording)
